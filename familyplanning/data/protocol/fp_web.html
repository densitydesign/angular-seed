harvesting the web corpus

We collected the first 100 Google.com results (in incognito mode) harvesting their URLs for each of the following queries: "Not having babies"; "Family size"; "Birth spacing"; "Fertility control"; "Fertility regulation"; "Birth control methods"; "Planned parenthood"; "Birth control"; "Preventing pregnancy"; "Rhythm methods"; "Pregnancy prevention"; "family planning"; "IUD "family planning""; "pill+"family planning""; "condoms "family planning""; "condoms contraception"; "pill contraception"; "hormonal cotraceptive"; "contraception".

Rogers, Richard. "The Googlization Question, and the Inculpable Engine."

cleaning and tagging the webpages

We qualified and tagged all the webpages accordingly to their contents and excluding from the corpus pages not concerned with Family Planning (i.e. PDF, Videos, etc.). The resulting taxonomy is composed by three main groups of categories, with a special focus on controversial issues.

Controversies, relates to sites that are more likely to show debate/controversy:

  • advocacy
  • development
  • education lifestyle/sexual health
  • family size
  • finances
  • human rights
  • law
  • politics
  • population growth
  • programme
  • religion

We have isolated other 2 subcategories within the controversial webpages:

Risk and Benefits:
  • Birth spacing
  • Cancer

  • others
  • VTE
Youth:
  • Capacity building

  • Law

Natural Family Planning related to site that present information on natural family planning, they are worthwhile exploring as they often highlights the risk of artificial methods and might have religious affiliations.

crawling the hyperlinks

We followed the web corpus hyperlinks to discovering other relevant URLs. From this new web corpus have been eliminated all the websites considered too general (i.e facebook, twitter) and the non relevant ones (i.e maps.google, youtube). Furthermore, also the websites with an in-degree index smaller than 3 have been excluded. For this step we used HYPHE a software developed at médialab | SciencesPo. Rogers, Richard. "Mapping public web space with the Issuecrawler." Digital cognitive technologies: Epistemology and the knowledge economy (2010): 89-99."

(visual) network) analysis

We examined through co-link / social networking analysis the network produced with the crawling. Furthermore, we analyzed visually the network using GEPHI, an open-source network analysis and visualization software package. Thanks to its force-based algorithm, ForceAtlas2, we produced the qualitative interpretation of the graph. Sébastien Heymann and Bénédicte Le Grand. “Exploratory Network Analysis: Visualization and Interaction” to appear in Hocine Cherifi (editor), Complex Networks and their Applications, Cambridge University Press.
